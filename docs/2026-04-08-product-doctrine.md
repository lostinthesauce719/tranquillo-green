# Tranquillo Green Product Doctrine

Tranquillo Green exists to reduce fear for cannabis operators by turning 280E, month-end close, and CPA handoff into a defensible operating system.

This is the filter for roadmap, design, copy, automation, and scope. If a feature does not make the books more defensible, the close more controlled, the handoff more trusted, or the operator more certain, it should not ship.

## What Tranquillo Green is

Tranquillo Green is a defensibility OS for cannabis finance.

It is built to:
- turn 280E from a black box into a decision machine
- orchestrate close work from intake through signoff
- produce evidence-backed CPA handoff, not messy exports
- automate repetitive accounting work without hiding logic
- reduce operator fear with visible status, proof, and next actions

In practice, the product should feel like:
- guided certainty during onboarding
- a killer import and review pipeline that gets dirty books into shape fast
- narrative workspaces that explain what happened, what changed, and what still needs attention
- CPA mode that packages support, assumptions, overrides, and reasoning in one place
- decision analytics that help users act, not admire charts

## What Tranquillo Green is not

It is not:
- a decorative dashboard business
- a generic SMB accounting suite
- a broad cannabis ERP with shallow modules everywhere
- an AI wrapper that speaks confidently without showing evidence
- an automation layer that posts, allocates, or closes books without visible controls
- a reporting product that stops at summaries and leaves the real work to email and spreadsheets

If a feature mainly makes the app look bigger, smarter, or more "platform-like" without improving defensibility or certainty, cut it.

## Trust principles

Trust is the product. Every critical workflow must make trust visible.

1. Show the why
- Every allocation, automation, flag, and recommendation must expose its logic, source data, and assumptions.

2. Show the evidence
- Outputs must link back to underlying transactions, documents, mappings, overrides, and operator actions.

3. Show the state
- Users should always know what is complete, what is blocked, who changed what, and what still requires judgment.

4. Keep humans in control
- Automation should prepare, suggest, reconcile, and package. Humans approve material decisions.

5. Make reversibility normal
- Overrides, reopens, and corrections are first-class. Fear drops when users know mistakes are recoverable.

6. Package trust for downstream stakeholders
- Controllers, founders, and CPAs should inherit context, not reconstruct it.

## Anti-bloat rules

1. No shallow modules
- Do not add categories like inventory, compliance, or settings unless they directly strengthen the 280E-close-handoff loop.

2. No dashboard filler
- A chart, KPI, or summary tile must drive a decision or reveal a risk. Otherwise it is decoration.

3. No vague AI surfaces
- Never ship "AI insights" without clear inputs, explicit reasoning, and bounded actions.

4. No hidden automation
- If the system changes records, allocations, statuses, or outputs, the user must be able to inspect the trigger, logic, and result.

5. No parallel workflows
- Do not create a second way to do the same critical job unless it serves a distinct role such as operator vs CPA.

6. No scope that weakens the wedge
- The wedge is cannabis accounting defensibility around 280E, close, and handoff. New work must deepen that wedge, not dilute it.

## Product standards

Every meaningful feature should meet these standards:

- Operational: tied to a real workflow step, owner, status, and next action
- Evidentiary: outputs trace back to source data and support
- Explainable: a smart user can understand why the system did what it did
- Reviewable: humans can approve, reject, override, or reopen
- Auditable: history of changes, actors, timestamps, and reasons is preserved
- Role-aware: operators, controllers, and CPAs see the right depth and controls
- Narrative: the workspace tells the story, not just the result
- Time-saving: meaningfully compresses close time, cleanup time, or CPA back-and-forth

If a proposed feature fails two or more of these, it is probably not core.

## Moat thesis

The moat is not generic bookkeeping software. The moat is trusted cannabis financial defensibility.

Tranquillo Green wins by combining:
- a purpose-built 280E decision engine
- a structured close orchestration layer
- an evidence graph connecting source data to outputs
- transparent automation with human control points
- CPA-ready handoff that preserves context, not just files
- a product experience built around fear reduction in a high-risk category

Over time, this creates compounding advantage:
- better import normalization for messy cannabis books
- richer decision history on allocations and overrides
- stronger support schedules and handoff packets
- more trust from operators, controllers, and external accountants
- harder-to-replicate workflow depth than generic accounting or AI tools

## Top execution priorities

1. Build the 280E decision machine
- rules, assumptions, allocations, overrides, support schedules, and visible reasoning

2. Make onboarding guided and certainty-first
- company setup, data intake, mapping, and initial close readiness should feel directed, not open-ended

3. Ship the killer import pipeline
- messy files in, mapped and validated records out, with row-level review and recovery built in

4. Turn close into orchestration
- one command center for readiness, blockers, ownership, signoff, and reopen paths

5. Make trust visible everywhere
- source links, audit history, reasons, evidence, and status must be present in every critical workspace

6. Nail CPA mode and handoff
- packages should include evidence, narrative, exceptions, overrides, and export history with minimal follow-up required

7. Add decision analytics, not vanity analytics
- surface bottlenecks, exception patterns, recurring cleanup work, and policy drift

8. Ruthlessly cut dead weight
- hide or remove modules, widgets, and flows that do not deepen the wedge

## Decision rule

When choosing between breadth and trust, choose trust.
When choosing between speed and defensibility, choose defensibility.
When choosing between impressive demos and operator certainty, choose certainty.

That is the product.
